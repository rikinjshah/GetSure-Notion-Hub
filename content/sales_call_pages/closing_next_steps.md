🎯 **Purpose of this phase**  
Convert interest into action by making the application process feel simple, logical, and urgent. This is where you transition from consultant to enrollment specialist, guiding them through the practical steps needed to get their protection in place.

The closing phase is often where agents get nervous and start overselling. Remember: if you've done your job well in the previous phases, the close should feel natural and inevitable. You're not convincing them to buy—you're helping them get what they've already decided they want.

Think of this phase as removing the final barriers between their current unprotected state and their desired protected state. Your job is to make the next steps feel easy, fast, and logical.

💭 **What the prospect might be thinking**  
"This makes sense, but is now the right time?" or "What if I'm making a mistake?" or "Will this be complicated?" Many prospects experience last-minute hesitation even when they want the protection. This is normal decision-making psychology, not necessarily objections to your product.

Others are wondering about the application process, how long approval takes, or whether they'll qualify. Some may be thinking about other people they should consult first.

🧠 **Why timing matters**  
People are most motivated to buy immediately after understanding the value and before they have time to overthink or get distracted by other priorities. The longer the gap between "I want this" and "I'm applying," the lower your close rate becomes.

Additionally, their emotional state right now—concerned about family burden, excited about protection—is ideal for decision-making. If they leave the call to "think about it," they return to their normal routine where this feels less urgent.

> **Callout**: The close isn't a high-pressure tactic—it's a natural conclusion to a logical conversation. If they want protection and can afford it, waiting only creates risk without benefit.

## 🎯 The Assumption Close Framework

### Step 1: Summarize Everything
*"[Name], let me make sure I understand your situation correctly. You're [age], you're concerned about [specific worry], you want [specific outcome], and the $X monthly investment works for your budget. Is that accurate?"*

### Step 2: Logical Next Step
*"Based on everything we've discussed, it sounds like this protection is exactly what you're looking for. The next step is to get your application started so we can get this coverage in place. Does that make sense?"*

### Step 3: Assume the Sale
*"Great! The application process is really straightforward—I can do most of it right over the phone. Let me get some basic information and we'll have you protected within a few weeks."*

## 💬 Trial Close Questions

Use these to test their readiness before going for the final close:

### Ownership Questions:
- "How do you feel about having this protection in place?"
- "Can you see how this would give you peace of mind?"
- "Does this feel like the right amount of coverage for your family?"

### Process Questions:
- "Are you ready to move forward with getting this protection?"
- "When would you want this coverage to start?"
- "Who would you like to name as your beneficiary?"

### Urgency Questions:
- "Is there any reason we shouldn't get this started today?"
- "What would need to happen for you to feel comfortable moving forward?"

## 🎭 Closing Scripts by Personality Type

### For the Analytical Decision-Maker:
*"Based on all the information we've reviewed—the coverage details, the pricing, the company ratings—does this check all your boxes? Great! Let's get the application process started so you can have this protection in place."*

### For the Relationship-Oriented Buyer:
*"I can tell how much you care about your family and want to protect them. This coverage ensures they'll never have to worry about funeral costs or feel burdened during an already difficult time. Are you ready to give them that gift of peace of mind?"*

### For the Results-Focused Buyer:
*"Bottom line: for less than $3 per day, you eliminate a $10,000+ problem for your family. That's what I call a smart investment. Let's get this done."*

### For the Security-Minded Buyer:
*"You've been responsible your whole life, and this is just one more way to be responsible for your family. The application takes about 10 minutes, and then you can relax knowing everything is taken care of."*

## 📋 Application Process Walkthrough

### Setting Expectations:
*"The application process is really simple. I'll ask you some basic information—name, address, date of birth, beneficiary information. Then there are about 8 health questions that are yes/no answers. The whole thing takes about 10 minutes over the phone."*

### Health Questions Preview:
*"The health questions are things like 'Have you been hospitalized in the past two years?' and 'Do you currently take more than 4 prescription medications?' They're designed to be simple and straightforward."*

### Timeline Explanation:
*"Once we submit your application, the insurance company reviews it within 2-3 business days. If you're approved—which most people are—they'll send your policy documents within about a week. Your coverage typically starts within 2-3 weeks from today."*

### Payment Setup:
*"We'll set up automatic monthly payments from your checking account, so you never have to worry about missing a payment. The first payment comes out about 30 days after your coverage starts."*

## 📊 Objection Prevention Chart

| Common Hesitation | Prevention Strategy | Response |
|------------------|-------------------|----------|
| "I need to think about it" | Create urgency | "What specifically do you need to think about? The coverage details, the price, or something else?" |
| "I want to discuss with family" | Offer three-way call | "That makes sense. When could we set up a call with them? I'm happy to explain everything." |
| "I'm not sure about the timing" | Emphasize protection gap | "Every day without coverage is a day your family is at risk. What would make the timing feel right?" |
| "What if I change my mind?" | Explain free look period | "No problem—you have a 30-day free look period. If you change your mind, you get a full refund." |

## 🚀 Creating Application Urgency

### Health-Based Urgency:
*"One thing I always tell my clients: the best time to apply for insurance is when you don't need it yet. Your health can change quickly, and once it does, this protection might not be available at these rates—or at all."*

### Rate-Based Urgency:
*"The rate I'm showing you today is based on your current age. These rates are guaranteed once you're approved, but if we wait, your next birthday could mean higher premiums."*

### Peace of Mind Urgency:
*"I know you requested information because this has been on your mind. Once we get this application submitted, you can stop worrying about this issue and focus on enjoying your life, knowing your family is protected."*

## 🎯 Alternative Closes for Hesitant Prospects

### The Puppy Dog Close:
*"Tell you what—let's go ahead and submit the application. You have 30 days to review everything once you receive the policy. If for any reason you decide it's not right, you can cancel for a full refund. That way, your coverage can start while you're thinking it over, rather than leaving your family unprotected."*

### The Ben Franklin Close:
*"I understand this is a big decision. Let's do what Benjamin Franklin used to do—make a list of pros and cons. On one side, write down the benefits of having this protection. On the other side, write down the benefits of waiting. What do you think that list would look like?"*

### The Alternative Choice Close:
*"I can see you want this protection—the question is just how much coverage feels right. Would you prefer to start with the $10,000 option for $67 per month, or go with the $15,000 option for $89 per month?"*

## 📞 Handling Final Hesitations

### "This is a big decision"
*"You're absolutely right—protecting your family is a big decision. That's exactly why successful people like you don't leave it to chance. The decision you're really making is whether to handle this responsibility now, while you're healthy and can get coverage, or leave it for your family to handle later."*

### "I've never bought insurance over the phone"
*"I understand that comfort level. What if I told you that you're not actually buying anything today? We're just submitting an application. The insurance company reviews your information, and you review their policy. You don't pay anything until you're completely satisfied with everything."*

### "I want to shop around"
*"That's smart consumer behavior, and I respect that. Let me ask you this: if you found the exact same coverage for $5 less per month somewhere else, would that change your life? Meanwhile, every day you're shopping is another day your family doesn't have protection. Sometimes the best deal isn't the cheapest price—it's getting the right coverage in place quickly."*

## ✅ Success Indicators

You know you're ready to start the application when:
- They're asking practical questions about start dates
- They mention beneficiary names or decisions
- They ask about payment methods
- They seem eager to "get this handled"

## 🚫 Mistakes to Avoid

- Rushing the close
- Over-explaining or overselling
- Ignoring last-minute hesitations
- Failing to set clear next steps
