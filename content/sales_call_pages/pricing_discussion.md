🎯 **Purpose of this phase**  
Transform the price from a "cost" into an "investment" that provides massive value relative to the problem it solves. The goal isn't to present the cheapest option—it's to help them see the price as reasonable, affordable, and actually a bargain compared to the alternative.

Pricing discussions are where many sales are won or lost. The key is framing: instead of presenting an expense, you're showing them how a small monthly investment protects against a much larger future expense. Your job is to make the monthly premium feel manageable while making the death benefit feel substantial.

Remember: they've already agreed they want the protection. Now you're simply showing them what that protection costs and helping them see it's affordable.

💭 **What the prospect might be thinking**  
"I hope this isn't more than I can afford" or "I wonder how this compares to what others pay" or "Is this a good deal?" Many prospects on fixed incomes worry about taking on new monthly expenses, even small ones.

Others are mentally comparing your price to other things they spend money on, trying to decide if this is worth it. Some may be wondering if they can get the same thing cheaper elsewhere.

🧠 **Why pricing psychology matters**  
The way you present price affects how it's perceived. A price presented as "$85 per month" feels different than "less than $3 per day" or "about what most people spend on coffee." The same exact price can seem expensive or cheap depending on your framing.

Additionally, people evaluate price relative to value. If you've done a good job showing the massive value of having this protection, the price will seem reasonable. If you haven't established value, any price will seem too high.

> **Callout**: Never apologize for the price or act like it's expensive. Your confidence in the value will translate to their confidence in the purchase.

## 🎯 The Value-First Pricing Framework

### Step 1: Reinforce the Problem Cost
Before showing your price, remind them what NOT having this protection costs:

*"Before I show you the monthly investment for this protection, let's remember what we're protecting against. The average funeral today costs $8,000-$12,000, and that's just for basic services. When you add burial costs, headstone, reception, and other expenses, families often face $15,000 or more in immediate bills."*

### Step 2: Frame as Daily Investment
Present monthly premiums in daily terms to minimize psychological impact:

*"For someone your age, this protection works out to about $2.85 per day. That's less than most people spend on coffee, but it provides $15,000 of guaranteed protection for your family."*

### Step 3: Show the Math
Make the value obvious with simple calculations:

*"Think about it this way: over 10 years, you'll invest about $10,200 in premiums. But your family is guaranteed to receive $15,000 tax-free whenever you pass away. Even if you live another 20 years, you're still getting a great return on your investment."*

## 💰 Pricing Presentation Scripts by Coverage Amount

### $10,000 Coverage Presentation:
*"For $10,000 of guaranteed coverage—which handles most funeral costs—the monthly investment is $67. That breaks down to about $2.20 per day, or roughly $16 per week. Most of my clients tell me that's less than they spend on lunch twice a week."*

### $15,000 Coverage Presentation:
*"For $15,000 of coverage—which covers funeral costs plus helps with other immediate expenses—you're looking at $89 per month. That's about $2.95 per day. When you consider that this guarantees your family won't face a $15,000 bill when they're grieving, most people find it very reasonable."*

### $20,000 Coverage Presentation:
*"For $20,000 of comprehensive coverage—funeral costs plus a cushion for other expenses—the monthly investment is $118. That works out to about $3.90 per day. Remember, this premium never increases, so in 10 years you'll still be paying the same amount while everything else gets more expensive."*

## 🎭 Addressing Price Concerns

### "That's more than I expected"
*"I understand—let's put this in perspective. You mentioned you're worried about leaving your family with funeral costs. The average funeral today costs $9,000, and that's just the basic service. This policy guarantees $15,000 for about $85 per month. Even if you pay premiums for 15 years, you'll have invested $15,300 but your family still gets $15,000 tax-free. And if something happens sooner, they get the full benefit even if you've only paid a few premiums."*

### "I need to think about fitting this in my budget"
*"That's smart financial planning. Let me ask you this: what would be easier for you—finding $85 per month now, or asking your family to find $10,000 all at once when they're dealing with grief? Most of my clients tell me that once they set up the automatic payment, they don't even notice it, but they sleep better knowing their family is protected."*

### "Can I get a smaller amount?"
*"Absolutely. Let's look at what different coverage amounts would cost and what they would cover."*

## 💡 Premium Justification Techniques

### The Coffee Shop Method:
*"You know what I find interesting? Most people spend $4-5 at Starbucks without thinking twice. That's $120-150 per month just on coffee. This protection costs less than that and provides $15,000 of guaranteed value for your family."*

### The Cable TV Method:
*"I bet you pay more for cable TV than this costs. Cable gives you entertainment for a few hours a day. This gives you and your family peace of mind 24/7 for the rest of your life."*

### The Car Payment Method:
*"Most people have a car payment of $300-500 per month for something that depreciates in value. This is less than $100 per month for something that guarantees $15,000 for your family. When you think about it that way, it's actually a pretty incredible deal."*

## 📈 Creating Urgency Around Pricing

### Age-Based Urgency:
*"One thing to keep in mind is that these rates are based on your current age. Every year you wait, the premiums go up. The rate I'm showing you today is the lowest rate you'll ever qualify for."*

### Health-Based Urgency:
*"Also, these rates assume you qualify for our standard health category. If your health changes, you might not qualify for these rates—or might not qualify at all. That's why I always recommend people get coverage while they're healthy and can get the best rates."*

### Market-Based Urgency:
*"Insurance rates have been increasing across the industry. The company I work with has held their rates steady, but there's no guarantee that will continue. The rate I'm showing you today is locked in once you're approved."*

## 🎯 Multiple Option Strategy

Instead of presenting one price, give them options:

### Three-Tier Approach:
*"Let me show you three options based on what you told me about your budget and coverage goals:*

- *Basic protection: $10,000 coverage for $67/month*
- *Standard protection: $15,000 coverage for $89/month* 
- *Comprehensive protection: $20,000 coverage for $118/month*

*Most people in your situation choose the standard option because it covers funeral costs plus gives the family some breathing room for other expenses. Which one feels right for your situation?"*

## ✅ Success Indicators

You know your pricing presentation is working when:
- They ask about payment options or when coverage starts
- They start doing their own math: "So that's only $20 per week?"
- They ask about different coverage amounts
- They mention how the price compares to other expenses

## 🚫 Mistakes to Avoid

- Leading with price before value
- Apologizing for the price
- Only showing one option
- Not giving context or comparisons

## ⏭️ Transition to Next Phase

"Based on everything we've discussed, does this feel like the right protection for your family? If so, the next step is to get your application started."
