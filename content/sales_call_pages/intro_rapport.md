🎯 **Purpose of this phase**  
Establish trust within the first 30 seconds and set the tone that this call is about **helping**, not selling. Make the client feel heard and respected.

The introduction phase is your one opportunity to make a first impression. Research shows that prospects form an opinion about you within the first 7 seconds of contact. Your tone, pace, and word choice in these opening moments will determine whether they engage openly or put up defensive walls.

💭 **What the prospect might be thinking**  
They might wonder: "Is this just another sales call?" or "Will this take too much time?" Adults over 50 have received countless sales calls and are naturally skeptical. They're also often dealing with fixed incomes and may feel vulnerable about financial decisions.

Many prospects at this stage are thinking about their own mortality for the first time, which can create anxiety. Your job is to acknowledge this sensitivity while positioning yourself as a trusted advisor, not just another salesperson trying to make a commission.

🧠 **Why it matters**  
Building rapport early reduces resistance later—and increases the chance they'll engage with the rest of the call. When people feel understood and respected, they're 3x more likely to share personal information that helps you serve them better.

The neuroscience is clear: when people feel safe, their prefrontal cortex (logical brain) stays engaged. When they feel threatened or pressured, their amygdala (fear center) takes over, making rational conversation nearly impossible.

> **Callout**: Use their name at least twice, **mirror their tone**, and **confirm availability** before proceeding. Names create instant familiarity, and mirroring builds subconscious connection.

## 💬 Suggested Phrases

**Opening Lines:**
- "Hi [Name], this is [Agent] from [Company]. How are you doing today?"
- "I saw you requested information about final expense coverage—is this still a good time to talk?"
- "Thank you for your interest in learning more about protecting your family from funeral costs."

**Availability Confirmation:**
- "Do you have about 10 minutes for me to explain how this works?"
- "I want to respect your time—would now work, or should we schedule something later?"
- "Before we dive in, is this a convenient time for you?"

## 🎯 Further Coaching Tips

**Vocal Technique:**
- Speak *slightly slower* than normal—this projects calm confidence and authority
- Lower your voice pitch by 10-15%—deeper voices are perceived as more trustworthy
- Pause for 2-3 seconds after asking questions—silence invites response

**Energy Matching:**
- Notice their energy level and match it: if they speak softly, do the same
- If they seem rushed, acknowledge it: "I can hear you're busy—this will be quick"
- If they sound tired, slow down and be more gentle in your approach

**Active Listening Signals:**
- Use verbal nods: "I understand," "That makes sense," "I hear you"
- Repeat back what they say: "So you're looking for something affordable..."
- Ask follow-up questions that show you're paying attention

## 📌 Quick Reference: Tone Matching Strategy

| Prospect Tone | Agent Match Strategy | Example Response |
|---------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Fast talker | Use brisk pacing | "Great! I'll keep this quick—need just 5 minutes." |
| Hesitant/Quiet | Slow and reassuring | "No rush at all—whenever you're ready is fine." |
| Skeptical | Acknowledge concern | "I understand—you probably get a lot of these calls." |
| Friendly/Warm | Match enthusiasm | "Wonderful! I'm excited to help you with this." |
| Business-like | Professional tone | "Perfect. Let me explain exactly how this works." |

## 🚨 Red Flags to Avoid

Never start with:
- "How are you today?" (too generic)
- "This will only take a minute" (usually false)
- "I'm not trying to sell you anything" (immediately puts sales in their mind)
- Jumping straight into product features without permission

## 🎭 Mindset for Success

Remember: You're not interrupting their day—you're potentially solving a problem they've already identified. They requested information because they have a genuine concern about leaving their family with financial burden.

Your confidence in this phase sets the tone for everything that follows. If you believe you're providing valuable service (which you are), that belief will come through in your voice and approach.
